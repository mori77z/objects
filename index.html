The Anguilla lamp is an example of a low-voltage lighting system that operates without conventional wiring to the light source. The 12V current is conducted through the metallic frame, eliminating the need for separate electrical cables. The frame is made entirely of conductive metal. This technique enables an elegant and minimalist design solution.

Plywood Stool (2023)

← →

This stool was made in the Molded Wood Workshop Class at OTH Regensburg. Made out of 21 sheets of natural Maple Veneer and is formed to fit the ergonomics of the human perfectly. The wood is only treatened with wood polish and still has a very natural look and good durability. I tried to hit the sweet spot within total minimalistic design and a ergonomic practical look.

Spider Chair (2023)

← →

The "Spider Chair" is an innovative piece crafted from a discarded Caligaris Munich chair frame.
This unique design repurposes materials by integrating old tree stumps and steel tubing, finished with a striking blue paint.
The chair's aesthetic combines the organic texture of wood with the sleekness of metal, resulting in a modern, functional, and eco-friendly seating solution.
The images depict various stages of the design process, from initial concepts to the final prototype, highlighting the chair's versatility and potential applications in different settings.

Frisk (2021)

← →

Small, handy purse made old tents. After many prototypes, I developed a final pattern that does not require any seams or gluing.
The purse can also be assembled and used from other materials such as an old LV bag.

The possibilities for color selection and different materials are therefore almost endless, making the Frisk wallet a real upcycling product.

The "Frisk" card holder with a coin compartment is crafted out of discarded tent floors.
There is no need for clue or stitching, still ensuring a durable and environmentally friendly design.
Its innovative pattern therefor allows for easy assembly through interlocking components.

Erdware (2024)

Material-centered design of a ceramic luminaire

← →

This project describes a speculative and alternative approach to the material ceramic. The focus is on the “Material-Driven Design” method and already established processing techniques, but also on the practical exploration of new possibilities and the consolidation of existing research results.  Through this approach, the work aims to contribute to the further development of knowledge and processing methods in the field of ceramic design. It also serves as a study of the potential of ceramic materials for other designers, both from an ecological and constructive perspective.

In my project developing a design of a ceramic object, I investigated ceramic materials and materials such as clay or porcelain and aimed to show a usecase. Driven by the process of Material-Driven Design, I focused on the principles of material research. In particular, I focused on the sustainable potential and a suitable application example through the design of the Leuchte 101.

Snowflake Trivet (2019)

Expandable trivet for different sizes

← →

This project describes a trivet made of a 3D-printed material and was developed for the production process of additive manufacturing. Its concept was developed for a family of products to be expanded by different arrangements.

The focus was on the unique structure of the snowflake, which can adapt according to size. The trivet can be used as a placeholder for different pot sizes and is adjustable to the desired shape.
