Spider Chair (2023)

The "Spider Chair" is an innovative piece crafted from a discarded Caligaris Munich chair frame. This unique design repurposes materials by integrating old tree stumps and steel tubing, finished with a striking blue paint. The chair's aesthetic combines the organic texture of wood with the sleekness of metal, resulting in a modern, functional, and eco-friendly seating solution. The images depict various stages of the design process, from initial concepts to the final prototype, highlighting the chair's versatility and potential applications in different settings.

Frisk (2021)

The "Frisk" card holder with a coin compartment is crafted out of discarded tent floors. There is no need for clue or stitching, still ensuring a durable and environmentally friendly design. Its innovative pattern therefor allows for easy assembly through interlocking components.

Small, handy purse made from the remains of old tents. After many prototypes, I developed a final pattern that does not require any seams or gluing. The purse can also be assembled and used from other materials such as an old LV bag. The possibilities for color selection and different materials are therefore almost endless, making the Frisk wallet a real upcycling product.

Standardized Furniture Concept (2023)

My project focuses on my innovative idea of developing a furniture series based on standard DIN components such as Euro perforated panels and C-rails. This approach not only enables considerable cost savings, but also creates a modular and durable furniture collection. By using standardized parts, the furniture is easy to rebuild and new configurations can be easily extended.
